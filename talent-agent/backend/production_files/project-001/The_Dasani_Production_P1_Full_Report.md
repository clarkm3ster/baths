## Production Brief

**Situation:** A child navigating homelessness in NYC, living in a shelter with her family while attending school, managing her siblings, and fighting for normalcy inside a system that processes her family as a case number.

**Challenge/Opportunity:** Building a dome around a child who is simultaneously inside the shelter system, the education system, the child welfare system, and the family court system — all of which operate independently. The dome must coordinate what these systems refuse to coordinate on their own.

**Key Systems:** homeless shelter system, public education, child welfare, family court, Medicaid, SNAP, TANF

**Flourishing Dimensions:** housing, education, family stability, health, creativity, autonomy, belonging

---

## Principal

**Ava DuVernay**

Vision: Her dome productions would center the person's experience of being inside the system looking out — the paperwork, the waiting, the explaining yourself again, the moment when coordination actually works.

Signature: Individual dignity within structural critique. The person is never an example — they're the protagonist. The system is the antagonist, not the subject.
