## Production Brief

**Situation:** Former textile factory, partially demolished, foundation and partial walls remain. The neighborhood's industrial heritage is written in the remaining brickwork. Currently a dumping ground but the community remembers what it was.

**Challenge/Opportunity:** The industrial shell provides structure and character. The mixed-use zoning allows creative programming. Could become a maker space, community workshop, performance venue, or hybrid. The ruins are an asset, not a liability.

**Zoning:** IRMX (Industrial Residential Mixed Use)
**Lot Size:** 22,000 sqft
**Constraints:** Environmental remediation required, Structural assessment of remaining walls, Loading dock access could enable logistics programming

---

## Principal

**Bureau Betak / Alexandre de Betak**

Vision: A vacant lot activated like a runway show — every arrival is choreographed, every reveal is timed, the space is transformed not by construction but by light, sound, sequence, and attention. The ephemeral becomes permanent through the experience it creates.

Signature: Attention as material. The sequence in which things are revealed changes what they mean. Raw space is the best canvas because it has no expectations. Transformation happens in the viewer, not the space.
