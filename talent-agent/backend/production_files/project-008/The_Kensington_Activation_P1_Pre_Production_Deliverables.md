## Permit Pathway Map: Santiago Reyes-Moreno

**Practitioner:** Santiago Reyes-Moreno (brownfield remediation)  
**Capability:** spatial legal  
**IP Domain:** policy  

### Referenced Work

- "Soil Memory" — Research on how industrial contamination histories affect community relationships to land (research, 2020)
- "The Growth Protocol" — Open-source methodology for converting contaminated urban lots to productive community space in 18 months (methodology, 2022)

### Deliverable

From the legal analysis, Santiago Reyes-Moreno now maps the permit pathway: every approval, timeline, and fee required to activate 2800 Kensington Ave. Drawing on "Soil Memory", the map shows that Structural assessment of remaining walls can be addressed through a variance process, while Loading dock access could enable logistics programming requires a phased approach. Every vacant lot has a memory in its soil. The pathway identifies the critical-path approvals — which permits gate others, and where parallel applications can save months.
