## Parcel Legal Analysis: Santiago Reyes-Moreno

**Practitioner:** Santiago Reyes-Moreno (brownfield remediation)  
**Capability:** spatial legal  
**IP Domain:** policy  

### Referenced Work

- "Soil Memory" — Research on how industrial contamination histories affect community relationships to land (research, 2020)
- "The Growth Protocol" — Open-source methodology for converting contaminated urban lots to productive community space in 18 months (methodology, 2022)

### Deliverable

Applying "Soil Memory" to 2800 Kensington Ave, Santiago Reyes-Moreno reads the IRMX (Industrial Residential Mixed Use) zoning code as a design document — what it permits, what it prohibits, and the variance pathways between. The 22,000 sqft lot in Kensington has a regulatory landscape shaped by its history: Former textile factory, partially demolished, foundation and partial walls remai. Key constraint: Structural assessment of remaining walls. Every vacant lot has a memory in its soil. The analysis maps every permit pathway and identifies 3 activation scenarios within the current code. [Bureau Betak / Alexandre de Betak's direction: Attention as material.]
